Canadian heritage

Hello and welcome to our webpage that focuses on canadian history from 1850-1890. on this wonderful page you will find info on major things that happened in between these dates ranging from Abraham lincoln's assassination to the fugitive slave act and the civil war. If there's any missing info that you think should be on this page please contact us at zed@aol.com

1850

Events
  • Malcolm Cameron visits Washington, D.C. about a trade reciprocity agreement
  • The St. Lawrence and Atlantic Railroad opens
  • In the United States, the Fugitive Slave Act is passed. It provides that even free persons can be made a slave if suspected of being a runaway. As a result, more fugitive slaves and free Black persons come to Canada
  • The site of John By's headquarters during the construction of the Rideau Canal is incorporated as Bytown
  • Gold discovered in British Columbia, and coal on Vancouver Island
  • The Canadian government passes the Railway Guarantee Act

    • 1851

    events
  • The first Canadian postage stamps are printed
  • Prince Edward Island wins responsible government
  • Harbor Commissioners deepen Lake St. Peter
  • The bloomer costume appears in Montreal
  • The Vancouver Island legislature meets for the first time
  • The St. L. and A. Railway is opened to Richmond
  • William Kennedy was commander of the second sponsored expedition to find Sir John Franklin
  • Gabriel Franchere's Narrative of a Voyage to the Northwest Coast of America published in Montreal
  • The United Kingdom transfers control of the colonial postal system to Canada

    • 1852

    events
  • Trinity College opens
  • Beginning of a fire which burns 11,000 houses in Montreal
  • The Bank of Montreal issues notes like the Bank of England's; denomination water-marked
  • The Toronto Stock Exchange opens
  • Laval's Seminaire du Quebec founds Université Laval, North America's oldest French Language university

    • 1853

    events
  • A description of the proposed bridge across the St. Lawrence is published
  • Gavazzi Riot in Quebec are quelled by military
  • The Grand Trunk Railway is opened to Portland
  • Investigation of the riot proceeds, at Montreal
  • Irregular calling of jurors delays trial for riot
  • The first Canadian railroad is built, running from Portland, Maine from Montreal.
  • The Grand Trunk Railway is chartered
  • Mary Ann Shadd becomes the first woman in North America to become editor of a newspaper. Working out of Chatham, Ontario, she publishes, edits and writes in the Provincial Freeman, a newspaper serving the Black community in Ontario
  • Russian explorer-trappers find oil seeps in Cook Inlet

    • 1854

    events
  • The Great Western Railway opens, linking Toronto, Hamilton and Windsor
  • The Canadian-American Reciprocity Treaty is signed
  • A Great Western Railway passenger train collides with the tail end of gravel train at Baptiste Creek, Canada West. At least 52 people are killed
  • Establishment of the African Baptist Association of Nova Scotia
  • St. Joseph’s Academy for Young Ladies, later to be known as St. Joseph's College School, was founded by the Sisters of St. Joseph of Toronto

    • 1855

    events
  • Bytown is renamed Ottawa
  • A bridge over the Niagara River near Niagara Falls is completed
  • Charlottetown incorporated as a city
  • The Charlottetown Police Department is established
  • Newfoundland general election, 1855

    • 1856

    events
  • Creation of the village of Embrun, Ontario
  • Formation of the British Methodist Episcopal Church (BME), an all Black church
  • The Grand Trunk Railway opens its Sarnia-Toronto-Montreal line

    • 1857

    events
  • The biggest Estonian newspaper Postimees is established by Johann Voldemar Jannsen
  • The partly French-owned London General Omnibus Company begins operating
  • The 7.9 Mw Fort Tejon earthquake shakes Central and Southern California with a maximum Mercalli intensity of IX (Violent)
  • The University of Calcutta is established in Calcutta as the first multidisciplinary modern university in South Asia. The University of Bombay is also established in Bombay, British India, this year
  • The National Deaf Mute College (later renamed Gallaudet University) is established in Washington, D.C., becoming the first school for the advanced education of the deaf
  • Federal Constitution of the United Mexican States promulgated
  • France and the United Kingdom formally declare war on China in the Second Opium War
  • The largest slave auction in United States history, dubbed as 'The Weeping Time'. Over a 2-day period (starting March 2), Pierce M. Butler sells 436 men, women, children, and infants, all of whom are kept in stalls meant for horses at a racetrack in Savannah, Georgia, for weeks beforehand
  • Panic of 1857: New York banks close and do not reopen until December 12
  • Queen Victoria chose Ottawa as the capital of Canada

    • 1858

    events
  • William I of Prussia becomes regent for his brother, Frederick William IV, who has suffered a stroke.
