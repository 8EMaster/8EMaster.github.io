About Canada

Canada is a North American country stretching from the U.S. in the south to the Arctic Circle in the north. There are a total of 3,427 cities, towns, and villages in Canada. This country is also home of Niagara Falls, which includes an excessive amount of famous waterfalls. The population of Canada is 35.16 million people as of 2013. The capital city of Canada is Ottawa. Also, this is a bilingual country which means they speak they speak English as a first language and French as a second. Majority of the country has cold climates, but southerly areas are warm in the summer.

The Founder of Canada

Canada was discovered in 1534 by, Jacques Cartier. Jacques Cartier is a French explorer who is mainly known for giving Canada its name and exploring the St. Lawrence River. He was born in 1491 and passed away in 1557. Also, Jacques was the very first European that was able to map and describe the shores of the Saint Lawrence River and the Gulf of Saint Lawrence. In the facts below it will give you a great understanding of how Canada was created from 1850-1890.

1850-1860.

  1. 1850: The site of By's headquarters during the construction of the Rideau Canal is incorporated as Bytown.
  2. 1854: Canada and the U.S ensure the reduction of customs duties by signing a Reciprocity Treaty.
  3. 1855: Bytown is renamed Ottawa.
  4. 1856: The Grand Truck Railway opens its Toronto-Montreal line.
  5. 1858: British Columbia receives Chinese immigrants from California.

1860-1870

  1. 1860: Abraham Lincoln became the 16th president of the U.S.
  2. 1861: Joseph Howe became the premier of Nova Scotia.
  3. 1867: The British North America Act is passed by the British parliament.
  4. 1867: John A MacDonald becomes the first prime minister of Canada.
  5. 1869: The red River Rebellion has begun.

1870-1880

  1. 1870: Manitoba becomes a province.
  2. 1871: British columbia joins confederation.
  3. 1873: Prince Edwards Island joins confederation.
  4. 1875: The first phone by Bell was revealed in Boston.
  5. 1878: The government bans Chinese workers from public works.

1880-1890

  1. 1880: Emily Stowe received a license to practice medicine in Toronto.
  2. 1885: Louis Riel was hanged in Regina.
  3. 1886: Vancouver was discovered.
  4. 1883: The Literacy Club of 1876 is replaced by the Toronto Woman’s Suffrage Association.
  5. 1887: Wilfred Laurier was chosen by the Liberals to be leader.
