Introduction

This website contains a timeline of Canada from 1850 - 1890, as well as a selection of videos, pictures, and other facts. Canada is a North American country with a population of 35 million, and has been an independent nation since 1867, when it was established. Some popular and famous Canadian cities include Toronto, Ottawa, Vancouver, Quebec, and Victoria Islands. Canada is also known for the Rocky Mountains, Niagara Falls, a wide variety of French speaking cities, poutine, maple syrup, beavers, the word “eh”, toques, and the overuse of the word “sorry”.

Chronology

  1. 1850 The Fugitive Slave Act is passed in the United States. It states that any escaped slave must be returned to their rightful owners. [2]
    2.  
  2. 1853 Mary Ann Shadd Edited the Provincial Freeman. She was the first black publisher in North America, as well as the first female publisher in North America. [3]
    3.  
  3. 1857 Ottawa is chosen as the new capitol of Canada by Queen Victoria, due to it being in the centre of Canada at the time, and provided the best defense against America. [8]
    4.  
  4. 1865 By this year, 100,000 slaves had escaped slavery and came to Canada using the underground railroad. [9]
    5.  
  5. 1866 - The Fenian Raids - A group of Irish Republicans attack British forces in Canada, hoping to take over Canada and use it as leverage for Irish independence. There a total of 3 raids against the British. Each raid had anywhere from 700 - 1,200 Fenian attackers. There are two more raids, one in 1870 and the last in 1871. The Fenian Brotherhood is left unsuccessful in all their attacks and fails to seize Canada with 14 Fenian casualties and 22 British casualties. [5]
    6.  
  6. 1867 - The Confederation of Canada; Canada leaves British rule and becomes the Dominion of Canada. [1]
    7.  
  7. 1869 Britain transfers control of their massive Rupert’s Land and North West Territories to Canada. [7]
    8.  
  8. 1870 Manitoba becomes the fifth province of Canada. [7]
    9.  
  9. 1871 British Columbia becomes the sixth province of Canada. [7]
    10.  
  10. 1873 Prince Edward becomes the seventh province of Canada. [7]
    11.  
  11. 1874 - Letitia Youmans founded the Women’s Christian Temperance Union with beliefs that alcohol abuse was the cause of unemployment, prostitution, poverty, and violence against women and children. They aim to make laws on alcohol stricter, or even outlaw alcohol all together. The movement is unsuccessful and fails to ban alcohol in Canada. [6]
    12.  
  12. 1875 Jenny Trout became the first women licensed to practice medicine in Canada. [8]
    13.  
  13. 1876 The Indian Act is passed by the Parliament of Canada, it contains various laws concerning the protection and security of indigenous people, especially around indigenous education systems. [7]
    14.  
  14. 1877 The Toronto’s Women’s Literary Club held it’s first meeting in Toronto. The group tackled many problems, including education, social policy, and women’s rights. It took over 40 years for women to achieve the right to vote. [8]
    15.  
  15. 1881 The Canadian Government authorizes and starts construction of the Canadian Pacific Railway. [7]
    16.  
  16. 1883 The Royal Canadian Regiment is founded. This is now known as the Canadian Army. [7]
    17.  
  17. 1885 The Last Spike - The Ceremonial final spike is driven into the Canadian Pacific Railway, signifying its completion. [4]
    18.  
  18. 1885 Louis Riel, president of the Provisional Government of Saskatchewan is hanged for treason, and leading the North-West Rebellion. [8]
    19.  
  19. 1886 The city of Vancouver is born with a population of around 1,000 residents. [7]
    20.  
  20. 1890 Vancouver reaches a population of 12,000. [7]
